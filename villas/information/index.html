ABOUT YOUR STAY

Check-in

Check-in time is between 3:00 pm and 9:00 pm.

If you plan to arrive later in the evening, please let us know so we can best prepare ourselves. You may request an early check-in. However, it cannot be guaranteed and is dependent on availability.

Check-out

Sadly, all good things come to an end.

Check-out time is at 11:00 am

You may request a late check-out until 12:00 pm. After that time late check-out fees apply.

Parking

We offer on-site free parking at each villa for a single small or medium-sized car. Larger vehicles, like vans, or additional vehicles can park for free in our general parking area near the entrance.

Traffic within OCAMA

We only allow cars and small vans (including taxis) to drive up to the villas and park there. It is not permitted to drive between villas. Only our service vehicles can go everywhere to provide services or move guests around.
Buggies, quads, motorcycles and noisy cars are banned and must remain in the general parking area, so as to not disturb the peaceful environment of OCAMA’s residences.

Beach Access

Each of the 8 villas is located between 6 and 15 minutes walk from Playa del Amor. A 5-minute walk from Playa del Amor over a rocky path takes you to Playa Breman, while a 7-minute walk in the opposite direction takes you to Playa Ermitaño.

If you're not in the mood to walk, you can call for a ride and one of our staff will give you a lift to our closest beach, Playa del Amor.

Meals

OCAMA is designed to meet the highest standards of luxury, service, functionality, aesthetics, and convenience. Our villas offer fully equipped kitchens that include a stove, oven, refrigerator, potable water dispenser, as well as cookware, utensils, and kitchen gadgets for effortless meal preparation.

Our Chef and his team prepare a variety of Dominican and international dishes on demand, delivered directly to your villa as room service.

Subject to availability, our Chef also offers private dinners featuring a tasting menu presented through a series of small courses. These dinners provide a unique blend of culinary and social experiences. Prices are detailed in our food menu.

Please note that meals and beverages are charged separately and are not included in the villa rate.

To help our chefs plan effectively for grocery shopping, meal preparation, and delivery, we kindly ask guests to inform us in advance of their meal preferences. Ordering times are specified on the menu.

Please also let us know if you have any dietary restrictions or food intolerances, so we are able to accommodate them accordingly.

For dining outside of OCAMA, there are several excellent restaurants in downtown Las Galeras and Samaná, a 25-45 minute drive away.

Shopping

OCAMA is located in a remote part of the island without supermarkets and with limited availability of supplies. Therefore we highly recommend you pass by a supermarket en route. If you come with one of our trusted taxi drivers, he will happily take you to one.

If you come on your own, we’ll provide you with the location of where to go shopping. We also offer a pre-arrival grocery shopping service for guests who are unable to do their own shopping.

If you forgot to pack an essential item, such as a toothbrush, razor, repellent, our small on-site shop, La Pulperia at OCAMA, will have them available for purchase to ensure your comfort. Most of the products we offer are eco-friendly, chosen carefully by our team to avoid the use of harsh chemicals or toxic components.

For cables and phone chargers, depending on the type of cable required, we might have them available for loan.

Leisure Activities

To ensure that you make the most of your stay and create even more memorable experiences, we offer concierge services and a digital and in-villa guidebook that will provide information about hotel amenities, nearby attractions, excursions, spa services, etc.

Let us know if you’re interested in any of these and we will be happy to send you more information.

Payment of Extra Services at Check-out

All services that we organize for you, including on-site meals and drinks, excursions, spa sessions, taxis, rental cars, and tips can be billed to your room and paid for at the end of your stay, using one of several digital payment methods. These include Airbnb Money Request, Zelle, and PayPal.

Depending on the payment method, surcharges may apply.

Tipping

We try to be as fair as possible with tips for our staff. All food and drinks coming from the kitchen are priced with a 10% tip included (by law), which goes directly to the kitchen staff. Any tips guests leave are distributed equally among the other employees, so both kitchen staff and all other staff get something for their efforts to provide our guests the best possible experience.

You can leave tips in cash or we can add them to your final invoice.

Las Galeras Cash Only

The smaller the town, the less electronic payment options are available. Las Galeras is very small, so be prepared to pay in cash everywhere, with the exception of a few businesses. Most stores and restaurants will accept dollars, but you will get a better deal if you pay in Dominican pesos.

Tolls

Guests who are driving themselves must have cash available for the tolls along the highways. Only Dominican pesos are accepted, and 2000 pesos bills are not accepted. Cards are not accepted.

At the time of this writing, the trip from Santo Domingo (airport or downtown) costs 460 pesos (approx. 8 USD) in tolls.

The trip from Punta Cana (airport or resort area) costs around 1000 (approx. 17 USD) pesos in tolls.

Currency Exchange

We do not offer currency exchange. We recommend exchanging some money for Dominican pesos when you arrive at the airport. Most malls and towns in the Dominican Republic have a Vimenca, Western Union or local bank who can offer currency exchange.

Luggage Storage

Should you arrive early or depart late, we can easily put all of your luggage in storage and allow you to take advantage of the facilities without the extra worries.

Power Adaptors

If you are coming from the United States, you won’t need a plug converter or power adapter. The DR uses the same outlets and 110V AC as the United States.

If you are coming from Europe, you will need a plug converter and maybe a power adapter. Many electronic devices, like laptops and phone chargers, work on both 110V and 220V.

However, other devices from Europe, like electric shavers and hair dryers, only work on 220V and will require a power adapter.

Wi-Fi

OCAMA retreat offers fast internet connections over optical fiber in all villas and several common outdoor areas, including Playa del Amor.

Our bandwidth and speed is perfectly adequate for most remote office work.

Electronic Devices

Part of the OCAMA concept is to promote connection with ourselves and the beautiful natural surroundings.

Our villas do not have televisions, but we offer an array of activities, such as hiking, beach hopping, excursions, and other amenities such as complimentary Wi-Fi, board games, books, yoga, and fitness equipment. Bring your own electronic devices as we have good internet connection.

Pet-Friendly/Policy

We welcome a maximum of 2 dogs per villa, weighing up to 25 pounds (12 kg). A pet fee of $200 per stay is added with a stay of up to five nights, while stays of six or more nights include an additional $200 deep-cleaning fee.

Please keep your dog on a leash when walking around the retreat. Your dog should never be left unattended in any part of the villa or the retreat. In the exceptional case of leaving your dog unattended in the villa, it must be locked in an appropriate kennel.

Please make sure your dog does not cause a disturbance by continuous barking. Also, make sure that you pick up your dog’s excrement anywhere on the property.

Housekeeping will only be performed if your dog is either properly kenneled or not present inside the villa.
Due to health regulations, dogs are not permitted in the pools.

Additional charges will apply should any damage occur, including scratch or bite marks, holes dug in the yard, and excessive staining on cushions or sheets.

No cats are allowed.

Health & Safety

As OCAMA is located in a remote area, far from large cities, we have taken several approaches to ensuring the health and safety of our guests.

Security

To ensure safety, peace of mind, and privacy of our guests, we offer round-the-clock security. We have security personnel, (several of whom patrol the retreat all day and all night), guarded entry points, and video camera surveillance.

First Aid

In the villas, you will find first aid kits with essential medical supplies in case of minor injuries or emergencies. The kits contain bandages, alcohol, antiseptic wipes, and other basic medical supplies to address common ailments or injuries.

Medications

Providing medications, even over-the-counter ones, could potentially lead to complications if a guest has an adverse reaction or if there are any misunderstandings about dosage or suitability for their medical condition. For this reason, at OCAMA, we only provide paracetamol and charcoal pills as over-the counter medication.

As we are in a remote area, we recommend guests bring any other medication they may need during their stay.

We also offer assistance in finding nearby pharmacies or medical facilities if guests require further medical assistance.

House Doctors

Should a guest require medical attention or additional medication, we offer house visits from local doctors, particularly for minor illnesses or injuries that do not require emergency care. These doctors can prescribe adequate medicine for any ailment that you may have.

Many of the doctors speak English. Alternatively, we can organize one of our own to join you during your doctor’s visit to ensure that you receive appropriate medical care with ease.

Medical Centers

Upon arrival you will find a guidebook with general information about OCAMA and Las Galeras. In this book you can also find emergency contact numbers and nearby medical facilities.

In the event of a medical emergency, we can assist with arranging an ambulance or other transportation to quickly and safely take guests to the nearest medical facility.

Wildlife Risks

The Dominican Republic, contrary to other tropical destinations, is not known for its poisonous animals, thus, encountering insects or small animals (frogs, lizards, spiders) will be more of a nuisance than a health risk.

We implement a strict pest control policy and offer daily housekeeping service in our villas to ensure minimal entry of insects or other pests into our villas. However, as we are surrounded by a lush forest we recommend bringing repellent, keeping food sealed, avoiding leaving dirty dishes or crumbs out to help deter insects or other animals from entering the villa. Additionally, keeping doors and windows closed can help prevent them from entering.

However, if you have concerns about specific insects or allergies, it's best to let us know.

To avoid mosquito bites that can potentially be dangerous, we recommend the use of insect repellent.

OCAMA offers a unique retreat experience where guests can disconnect from the modern world and reconnect with nature in a luxurious and caring environment.
